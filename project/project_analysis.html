Project Analysis

Web Standards Project, March 2013

Full Sail University

  • Client: Fat Wreck Chords
  • Designer: Gaston Kurtz
  • Web Address: http://www.fatwreck.com

Site Intent

Fat Wreck Chords is a record label. It uses its website to promote records/bands, connect with fans, and sell merchandise. The goals of the end users would be to buy merchandise, send in demos for consideration, find out tour dates and find interesting info on their favorite bands. The current site design is highly cluttered and difficult to navigate. The re-designed site needs to have a focus on simplicity, while still maintaining the grunge/edgy/who-cares-what-you-think sense of style presently found in the site.

Competitive Analysis

The FAQ on Fat Wreck Chords current site makes it clear that they have no interest in being a part of any large associations or organizations - apparently they prize their independence highly. Many of the competing small labels researched, such as ABB Records, 2B1 Multimedia, and Absolutely Kosher feature web presences that are not well designed. ABB Records perhaps has the best site out of these, with a clean-cut and simple to use interface, as well as an easy way for aspiring artists to upload demo tracks. The drawback to their site is a main landing/portal page that has very little real content, other than links to their store and to their dropbox for demo tracks - this will make it difficult for search engines to find them properly. The usability of their interface is one area where they definitely excelled above Fat Wreck Chords. Absolutely Kosher Records' site presents an appealing color palette, whereas Fat Wreck Chords contains many poor color and contrast choices, making it difficult to look at and decipher. None of these sites generates high traffic volume.

Targeted Audience

The current main demographic choosing to visit the Fat Wreck Chords site consists of males between the ages of 18 and 34. The typical user will likely be someone who identifies with the culture and unique personalities presented by the Fat Wreck Chords owners/operators. This user will also enjoy the local indie music scene and many will have an active nightlife, and will be visiting Fat Wreck Chords with the goal of finding good music connections for enjoyment.

Another user could be an aspiring artist or band who wants to get signed by a record label. They might not necessarily care who signs them, so they will likely be shopping various labels' websites and submitting numerous demo tracks in an effort to get their music "out there."
